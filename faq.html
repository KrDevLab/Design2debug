FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Common questions before starting a project. Can't find yours? Send it over.

What do you actually build — apps, sites, or both?

Both. Most projects are Flutter Android apps or marketing/product websites, plus the UI/UX design behind them. One person handles design, build, and debugging — nothing gets lost in handoff.

How long does a typical project take?

Small sites and single-screen apps usually run 2–3 weeks. Fuller apps or multi-page products run 4–8 weeks depending on scope, with an estimate shared before anything starts.

What's the process from first message to launch?

A short discovery chat, then a scope and quote. Once approved: design screens, a build phase with check-ins, then testing and launch — with working screens shown early.

How much does a project cost?

It depends on scope and screen count. Send a short description of what you're building and you'll get a fixed quote, not an hourly guess.

Do you offer support after launch?

Yes — every project includes a fixed window of post-launch fixes at no extra cost, with ongoing support available on a simple monthly or per-request basis after that.

How many rounds of revisions are included?

Two structured rounds of feedback are built into every design and build phase. Extra rounds are quoted separately, but small tweaks along the way are handled as normal.

What do you need from me to get started?

A rough idea is enough — a sentence, a sketch, or a link you like. Existing assets or copy help speed things up, but nothing polished is required to start.
